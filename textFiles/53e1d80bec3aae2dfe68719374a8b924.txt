[edit] Geographical

The Iron Triangle (Vietnam), the name U.S. forces in the Vietnam War gave to the Communist stronghold region northwest of Saigon

The Iron Triangle, a 1989 movie set in the Vietnam war


The Iron Triangle (Korea), used during the Korean War to describe an area in Korea bounded by Ch'orwon, Kumhwa, and Pyonggang. It straddles the central part of the Korean Demilitarized Zone.
Iron Triangle, Richmond, California, a neighborhood bounded on its three sides by railroad tracks
Willets Point, Queens, New York, known as "Iron Triangle" for its metal works
The Iron Triangle, a name given to the iron-producing region of South Australia, bounded by the towns of Port Augusta, Port Pirie and Whyalla


[edit] Political

Iron triangle, a concept in U.S. politics which refers to a three-sided relationship between Congress, a Federal department or agency, and a particular industry: especially, the Military-industrial complex

The Iron Triangle: The Politics of Defense Contracting, a 1981 book by Gordon Adams
The Iron Triangle: Inside the Secret World of the Carlyle Group, a 2003 book by Dan Briody


"Iron Triangle", a term used to refer to three core members of President George W. Bush's political inner circle: Karl Rove, Joe Allbaugh, and Karen Hughes
"Iron triangle", a term used to describe the government of occupied Japan under the Supreme Commander of the Allied Powers after World War II, with the Liberal Democratic Party (Japan), keiretsu, and the bureaucracy forming the iron triangle
More generally, any self reinforcing power structure, whether intentional or accidental, formal or informal.
