^  An Emacs module named EUDC extends Gnus/VM (View Mail) and BBDB to support LDAP.
^  KDE supports Newsgroups (NNTP) by the use of KNode
^  KDE supports feeds by the use of Akregator
^  KDE supports iCal by the use of KOrganizer
^  Studio Blog Reader is one RSS reader application for IBM Lotus Notes. IBM Lotus Notes version 8.x introduced a native RSS reader.
^  Support of feeds is available in Microsoft Office Outlook 2007.
^  Microsoft Outlook support of iCalendar varies by version.
^  Selective Download filters can skip/delete messages on POP3 server based on header (sender, recipient, subject, etc) information, this doesn't require to retrieve the full message body from the server.
^  Message Dispatcher allows the user to decide which messages should be retrieved, deleted or skipped based on the header information (sender, recipient, subject, etc) displayed to the user.
^  iCalendar is supported with an extension.
^  RSSyl plugin is required to read RSS and ATOM in Claws Mail.
^  vCalendar plugin is required to handle iCalendar in Claws Mail.
^  The BAT! requires MyGate plugin installed for handling NNTP.
^  The BAT! requires rss2mail or rss2pop3 plugin installed for handling RSS and ATOM.
^  i.Scribe / InScribe requires a plugin to handle LDAP.
^  Pegasus Mail can convert Newsgroup messages and/or RSS feeds to e-mails through the use of free add-ons.
^  Cone has an option to either keep all POP3 mail on the server, or download all messages from the POP3 mailbox.
